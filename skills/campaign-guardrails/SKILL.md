# Campaign Guardrails

These rules bind every agent on this project exactly as they bind the orchestrator. No deliverable that violates them is final.

## MESSAGE DISCIPLINE (WHAT KEEPS THIS LOUD AND LAWSUIT PROOF)

1. Facts and opinion stay split. Documented practice is stated as fact with a source. Characterizations, the trap, the forever contract, the frogs in slowly warming water, are framed as Michael's commentary. Commentary is protected speech and it lands hardest when the facts beside it are bulletproof.
2. The coordination question is investigated, not asserted. Never state as fact that carriers and handset makers secretly coordinate unless a document proves it. What is provable today is already damning: every major carrier independently runs the same 36 month conditional credit structure, their own published terms prove it, and carrier and handset maker incentives visibly align around locked in upgrade cycles. Publish that. In clearly labeled commentary, Michael can say what he sees, including that it looks like scheming to him, as long as the true, sourced facts sit right beside the opinion. The library treats coordination as an open investigative question: if litigation, regulatory findings, or documents ever show actual coordination, publish it with the receipts.
3. Aim at practices and companies, never at individual employees. No store staff, no low level names, ever.
4. Real numbers only. Publish counts the data supports, with the methodology public. Never inflate, never extrapolate without labeling it an estimate and showing the math.
5. No synthetic amplification. No fake accounts, no bots, no purchased engagement, no coordinated inauthentic anything. Growth is organic or it is nothing.
6. Michael's own AT&T dispute stays out of all platform content while it is pending, and campaign pushes never sync to his settlement events.
7. Corrections get fixed fast and visibly. A public corrections page is part of the site from day one.

## DATA RULES

1. Public and API-first sources only. Respect each platform's terms of service and robots.txt. If a source cannot be collected cleanly, drop it and log why.
2. Every record stores its source URL, capture date, and excerpt. Every public claim must trace to a record.
3. Aggregate, do not profile. Location is kept at city, state, or ZIP level for mapping. Never compile personal information about individual complainants. User submitted stories require consent, get moderated, and personal identifiers are scrubbed before anything displays.
4. Verify dataset structure before building on it. Candidate sources to check first: the FCC consumer complaint public dataset on opendata.fcc.gov, FCC ECFS dockets and rulemaking records, FTC cases and press releases, the CFPB complaint database API where device financing complaints land there, CourtListener and RECAP for litigation, state attorney general press releases and multistate settlements, BBB public complaint pages, the Reddit public API for carrier subreddits, and news coverage. Confirm current fields, location granularity, licensing, and rate limits for each before ingest.
5. Archive everything. Every cited page gets a Wayback Machine save and a local snapshot at capture time. Carrier promo pages and offer terms get snapshotted on a schedule with stored diffs so changes are documented over time. A source of truth cannot depend on links that die.
