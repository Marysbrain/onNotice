# Corrections Policy

> Draft for founder review. Not legal advice. A lawyer should check this before it goes live.

**Plain summary (three lines):**
When we get something wrong, we fix it fast and in the open.
We put a note on the corrected page and a dated entry on our public corrections page.
We do not quietly change a substantive claim and pretend it was always that way.

## How to report an error

If you think we got something wrong, tell us. Email us at corrections@carriersonnotice.com.

Tell us what page it is on and what you think is wrong. If you can, point us to a source.

## What happens when a claim is found wrong

When we confirm a claim is wrong:

- We fix it fast.
- We fix it visibly. We do not hide the change.
- We put a note on the corrected page saying what changed and when.
- We add an entry on our public corrections page.

## The corrections page

We keep a public corrections page from day one.

It lists every substantive correction we make, with dates. Anyone can read it.

A substantive correction is one that changes a fact or a claim, not a typo or a small wording fix.

## What we do not do

We do not silently edit substantive claims. If we change what we said about a fact, we say so. We do not rewrite the record and act like nothing happened.

## Why we do this

Getting things right matters more than looking right. An honest, visible fix is how we keep trust.
