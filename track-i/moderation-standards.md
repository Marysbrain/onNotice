# Moderation Standards

> Draft for founder review. Not legal advice. A lawyer should check this before it goes live.

**Plain summary (three lines):**
These are the public rules for which stories get published, held, or turned down.
Every story is read by a person, scrubbed of anything that identifies someone, and checked that it fits.
We log our moderation decisions, and we have a clear way to handle disputes about a published story.

## What gets a story published

A story can be published when it meets all of these:

- It is a first person experience. The person telling it lived it.
- It is on topic. It is about a carrier device promotion or a related practice.
- It does not name a store employee or any individual worker.
- It does not dox anyone. No exact addresses, no phone numbers, no private details about a person.
- It is plausible on its face. It reads as a real, coherent account.

## What gets a story held for review

A story is held, not published yet, when:

- Something needs checking before we can stand behind it.
- The facts are unclear and we want to confirm them.
- It may fit the rules but needs a scrub or an edit first.
- We have reached out to the submitter to verify and are waiting to hear back.

Held does not mean rejected. It means not yet.

## What gets a story rejected

A story is rejected when:

- It is not a first person experience.
- It is off topic.
- It names or targets an individual worker.
- It tries to dox or expose a person.
- It is not plausible, or it looks made up.
- It is abusive, hateful, or unlawful.

## How we scrub identifiers

Before any story is displayed, we remove things that identify the submitter or anyone else. That means:

- Names of individuals.
- Account numbers.
- Exact street addresses.
- Phone numbers and other direct contact details.
- Anything else that points to a specific private person.

Location, if shown, is kept at the city, state, or zip level only.

Contact info a submitter gives us is never displayed.

## How we handle disputes about a published story

If someone believes a published story is wrong, they can tell us at corrections@carriersonnotice.com.

When we get a dispute:

- We look at the story again.
- We check it against our records.
- If it is wrong or cannot be supported, we correct it or take it down.
- We handle it under our corrections policy, in the open.

We aim at practices and companies. We do not target individual workers. If a dispute is about an individual being named, we act quickly.

## We log our decisions

We keep a log of moderation decisions. That log helps us stay consistent and lets us review how a decision was made.
